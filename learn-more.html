Why Recycle?

Learn about [some] of the reasons recycling is so important. Informaiton gathered from the EPA and Keep America Beautiful :)

Recycling saves energy

Conservation of energy is the key element for the fight against climate change. Recycling saves energy because the manufacturer doesn’t have to produce something new from raw natural resources. By using recycled materials we save on energy consumption, which keeps production costs down.

Saves Space in Landfils

Recycling reduces the need for more landfills because we are able to reduce the amount of material filling them. Recyclable materials like glass and plastic can take hundreds of years to break down which means the landfils will continue to grow.

Recycling conserves natural materials

Recycling helps conserve important natural materials and protects these habitats for the future. All the new products are made from raw materials which are procured from harvesting the earth’s natural resources. E.g. paper and wooden products are cut from trees.

Recycling is good for the economy

Greater demand, means greater supply. Recycling and purchasing recycled products creates a greater demand for more recycled goods. Goods made from recycled materials use less water, creates less pollution and uses less energy. Companies rely on recycling programs to provide the raw materials they need to make new products.

Recycling saves you money

When you recycle or repurpose old products and have less dependency on new products and containers. We like to reuse our bottles for homemade cold-pressed juices. Check with your local food trucks or cafes, some of them may encourage the use of bringing in your own bowl or container and credit you. The same with bringing your own shopping bag to your local grocery shop.

Increases mindfulness

Each time you place recycleables in a recycling bin - as opposed to the trash can - you are making an intentional choice. As you continue to practice, you begin to notice others also recycling (or not). By being aware of our consumption of recyclable products and how we’re sorting for waste will flow into other areas of your life where you may be wasting time or energy carelessly.

You are not alone!

If you have your doubts about recycling, it's safe to say a large percentage of people feel the same. A study in 2017 done Keep America Beautiful shows that not everyone is confident that their recyclables are handled the way they should be.
