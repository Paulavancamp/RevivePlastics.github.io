Earth Week!

It’s earth week and there are so many ways to celebrate. Although, sustainability is advertised as a way to reduce your negative impact, it is also about creating a positive impact. “Going green” can be cutting out waste, driving less, using reusable bags, etc. – most of the things we’ve discussed this semester- and planting trees, starting a new beehive, cleaning up trash, etc. Before Earth Week comes to a close, there are still many opportunities to create some positive change and connect with eco-organizations. Below are a few FREE events for this weekend:


Friday April 26
Paint your own Reusable Bag!
707 Hub
2pm-5pm
Come to the hub and paint a bag you will be proud to tote around! Make it one of a kind. Bags are sold out, but you can still bring your own to paint!
Pedal Power movie
Annex
8pm-11pm
Students for an Environmentally Active Campus (SEAC) is showing Jurassic Park tonight using only power from stationary bikes. Sit, spin, and enjoy the show!

Saturday April 27
Urban ecology center day of service
All UEC Locations: Washington Park, Riverside Park, Menomonee Valley
9am-12pm
Get your hands dirty and help restore the Milwaukee waterways! Groups at the centers are partnering with Milwaukee Riverkeeper to pick up trash and plant native species in the parks. Gloves and Bags provided.
Rock the Green
Estabrook Park, Picnic Area 8
12pm-2pm
RAIN, SNOW, OR SUN, the show will go on!
Details from the website: “Join Rock the Green for the 8th Annual (and FREE) Earth Day Celebration Presented by Milwaukee Riverkeeper at Picnic Area 8 in Estabrook Park, Milwaukee. The #EarthDay celebration is a great way to learn about eco-friendly opportunities and support the community in its environmental effort” there will be food, music from Lex Allen and Chicken Wire Empire as well as eco-education from local environmental non-profits and organizations.”
